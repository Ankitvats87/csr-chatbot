| **Objective of the Project**                                                                                                           |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| The objective of the "Support for CT Scan Machine at Safdarjung Hospital, New Delhi" project is to enhance the capabilities of the Department of Radiodiagnosis and Interventional Radiology. The CT scan machine is a vital tool for diagnostic imaging, serving a large number of patients who rely on the hospital's services. Safdarjung Hospital, located in the heart of New Delhi on Ring Road, is the largest central government hospital in India, with a capacity of 3,000 beds. It is affiliated with Vardhman Mahavir Medical College and serves as a key healthcare provider, offering comprehensive care across more than 15 medical specialties. Currently, the hospital has only one CT scan machine, which is primarily used by critical departments such as Trauma, Emergency, and Stroke care. This limited capacity often leads to significant delays in diagnostic procedures, resulting in the loss of crucial time for patients in need of immediate care. With an average daily footfall of 15,000 to 18,000 patients, the addition of a second CT scan machine would greatly alleviate waiting times and enable the hospital to serve a larger number of patients more efficiently.
